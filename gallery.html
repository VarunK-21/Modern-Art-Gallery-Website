Gallery

Curated works by renowned artists, aligned to our vintage‑modern aesthetic.

Portrait of Adele Bloch‑Bauer I — Gustav Klimt (1907)

The pinnacle of Klimt’s Golden Phase—gold leaf, Byzantine ornament, and modern elegance around the patron Adele Bloch‑Bauer.

Tableau no. 1 — Piet Mondrian (1913)

A Cubist transition work—interlocking planes in greys and ochres foreshadow Mondrian’s later strict geometry.

Composition VII — Wassily Kandinsky (1913)

A vast, musical abstraction—surging color and line suggest themes of deluge, resurrection, and renewal.

Cat With Red Fish — Henri Matisse (c. 1914)

Decorative color and bold contour—Matisse turns a playful domestic scene into pure, radiant pattern.

The Great Wave off Kanagawa — Katsushika Hokusai (1831)

Ukiyo‑e masterwork—cresting wave and distant Fuji balanced in a perfect, dynamic composition.

Starry Night — Vincent van Gogh (1889)

Expressive swirls and radiant stars above a sleeping village—emotion and vision fused on the night sky.
